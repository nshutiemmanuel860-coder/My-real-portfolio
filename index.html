My Story

My name is NSHUTI Emmanuel, an IT student at King David Academy in Rwanda. My journey into technology began with a simple curiosity about how computers work and how software is created. This curiosity quickly turned into a passion that drives my daily learning and practice.

I started with basic computer skills and gradually moved to programming. My first programming language was C, which taught me the fundamentals of logic and problem-solving. From there, I expanded to C++ and discovered the world of object-oriented programming.

As I progressed, I realized the importance of web technologies and started learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This opened up opportunities to create interactive and visually appealing websites. My current focus includes mastering front-end development while also exploring back-end technologies.

Beyond programming, I developed a strong interest in cybersecurity. The challenge of protecting systems and data while understanding how they can be vulnerable fascinates me. I believe ethical hacking skills are essential for any software developer in today's digital landscape.

My goal is to become a full-stack developer with cybersecurity expertise, creating secure and efficient applications that solve real-world problems.
